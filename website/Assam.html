Welcome to Assam, where a vibrant mosaic of culture, gastronomy, heritage, allure, and wilderness awaits, cradled by the mighty Brahmaputra. Immerse yourself in the dynamic fabric of Assamese culture, where timeless customs interweave with contemporary expressions, crafting a narrative of resilience and festivity. Delight your palate with the diverse palette of Assam's cuisine, from the fiery zest of bhut jolokia-infused delicacies to the subtle sweetness of pithas, each bite a passage through the region's culinary legacy. Engage with the pulsating pulse of Assam's traditions, where the cadence of Bihu dances and the hymns of Satras resonate amidst mist-kissed hills. Marvel at the natural splendor of the land, where verdant tea estates sprawl endlessly, and lush forests harbor a wealth of biodiversity. Explore the serene allure of Assam's rivers, where ancient rituals entwine with the gentle flow of water, beckoning you to embark on a voyage of contemplation and rejuvenation. In Assam, every moment is an evocative fusion of tradition and ingenuity, offering a glimpse into the spirit of the Northeast.



My favourite food: "Paror Mangxo (Pigeon Meat Curry)"

A place must to visit: "Kaziranga National Park"

Best time to visit: November to april (park remains closed between may to october)


Fun Fact: Assam is renowned worldwide for its tea production, but what many may not know is that it is home to the world's oldest tea bushes. The indigenous Assamica variety of the tea plant, Camellia sinensis var. assamica, is believed to have originated in the Assam region thousands of years ago.
